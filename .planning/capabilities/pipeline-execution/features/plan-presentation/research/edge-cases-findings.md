### Boundary Conditions

- **Exactly 3 iterations consumed, still unresolved findings**: Step 8.5 enforces max=3; at this limit, plan.md says "surface for manual resolution." - **Plan is single auto task with no dependencies (wave=1, depends_on=[])**: Visual flow for this plan is a single box. ASCII flow diagram is a degenerate case: "Task A" with no arrows. ### Known Issues in Ecosystem

- **AskUserQuestion sequential fatigue**: User research on LLM-mediated decision workflows consistently shows that approval quality degrades after the 5th sequential decision prompt. Batching unrelated findings into a single question significantly improves decision quality. — [First principles: cognitive load research on sequential decision-making; no specific URL, but the finding is reflected in UX design guidance that limits wizard steps to ≤5 before requiring a break or summary]

- **ASCII diagram fragility in LLM output**: LLM-generated ASCII art is notoriously inconsistent — box widths vary, arrow alignment breaks with variable-length labels, and Unicode box-drawing characters may not render correctly in all terminals. Markdown code-block wrapping mitigates some rendering issues but does not fix alignment. — [First principles: LLMs generate ASCII one token at a time without spatial awareness of prior characters on the same line; character-counting is unreliable under autoregressive generation]

- **Instruction drift in multi-turn planner re-spawns**: When a planner is re-spawned with "prior justification" context and "update your plan based on feedback," the model may blend the prior justification with the new guidance rather than cleanly replacing contradicted claims. This is a known LLM instruction-following failure mode for complex structured updates. — [First principles: LLMs optimize for token-level coherence; a prior justification in context creates a "gravity" toward reproducing it even when instructed to revise specific parts]
