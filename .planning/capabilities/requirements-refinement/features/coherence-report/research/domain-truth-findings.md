## Domain Truth Findings

### First Principles

- **Multi-document synthesis by LLMs produces hallucinated content at alarming rates, and the rate does not decrease linearly with fewer documents.** Research on multi-document summarization shows up to 75% of LLM-generated summary content can be hallucinated (conversation domain) and up to 45% (news domain). Critically, increasing input documents from 2 to 10 produces only marginal changes in hallucination rate (plus/minus 5%), meaning the coherence-report's synthesis of N finding cards faces a baseline hallucination risk that is roughly constant regardless of finding count. The most common error types are pedantic/generic insights and instruction inconsistency -- not outright fabrication (0-9%). This means the primary risk for coherence-report is not invented findings but rather overly generic recommendations that don't add value beyond what the raw findings already say. — [https://arxiv.org/html/2410.13961v1]

- **Symptom-to-root-cause clustering is fundamentally a semantic grouping problem, not a filtering problem.** Multiple surface-level symptoms can trace to a single root cause, and two findings with different wording may describe the same underlying issue. Naive deduplication (string matching, simple comparison) yields problem counts at least one order of magnitude larger than proper root-cause clustering (Igor, CCS 2021). LLMs are well-suited for semantic clustering -- they can recognize that differently worded errors refer to the same underlying issue -- but only when explicitly instructed to cluster rather than enumerate. Without explicit clustering instruction, LLMs default to listing symptoms rather than synthesizing root causes. — [https://hexhive.epfl.ch/publications/files/21CCS.pdf; https://rootly.com/sre/unlock-faster-rca-with-rootly-advanced-clustering-algorithms]

- **Resolution ordering of interdependent issues must follow topological dependency, not just severity ranking.** A high-severity issue that depends on resolving a lower-severity issue first cannot be prioritized ahead of its dependency. This is the same constraint as software package installation: a package can only be installed when all its dependencies have been installed. The coherence-report's resolution sequence (FN-02, section 5) must combine severity/goal-alignment ranking with dependency ordering, or it will recommend an infeasible execution order. — [First principles: topological sort is the only correct ordering for a DAG of dependent tasks. Severity is a secondary sort key within dependency-valid orderings. This is well-established in both compiler theory (instruction scheduling) and project management (critical path method).]

- **Contradiction detection across documents is high-precision but low-recall for LLMs.** Claude-3 Sonnet achieves 71% accuracy on contradiction detection with chain-of-thought prompting. All models show "high precision but lower recall" -- they are conservative in predicting contradictions. Pairwise contradictions (between two documents) are easiest to detect (~89% accuracy), while self-contradictions and conditional contradictions are much harder (~45%). This means coherence-report's contradiction section (FN-02, section 6) will likely underreport contradictions rather than fabricate them. The downstream risk is false confidence -- users may assume no contradictions exist when the model simply failed to detect them. — [https://arxiv.org/html/2504.00180v1]

### Universal Constraints

- **Hallucinations cluster toward the end of generated output, not uniformly distributed.** Research shows "insights positioned earlier in the summary more likely to be accurate than those located later." For a 6-section RECOMMENDATIONS.md, this means the executive summary and root causes sections will likely be more accurate than the resolution sequence and contradictions sections at the end. This is a structural constraint on any single-pass synthesis: later sections have higher error rates. — [https://arxiv.org/html/2410.13961v1]

- **Categorical scoring is more reliable than numeric scoring when LLMs are the evaluator.** LLMs produce widely varying numeric scores across trials even with controlled prompts, due to non-deterministic generation. Binary and categorical outputs produce more stable and reliable evaluations. The TC-02 decision to use categorical goal alignment (blocks/risks/irrelevant) rather than numeric scores is validated by this research. Extending this: any confidence levels in the Q&A agenda (FN-03) should also be categorical, not numeric. — [https://medium.com/@pradeepdas/rethinking-llm-evaluations-from-numeric-scores-to-categorical-classifications-e823855dcc4e; https://www.kinde.com/learn/ai-for-software-engineering/best-practice/llm-as-a-judge-done-right-calibrating-guarding-debiasing-your-evaluators/]

- **Information summarization before diagnosis improves LLM root-cause analysis quality.** Meta's incident response research and LLM-based RCA literature both confirm that feeding raw diagnostic data directly into an LLM without prior summarization degrades reasoning quality. The landscape-scan's structured finding cards (FINDING-*.md) already serve as pre-summarized inputs, which is the correct architecture. If the coherence-report were to read raw capability files directly (bypassing findings), synthesis quality would degrade. — [https://www.tryparity.com/blog/how-meta-uses-llms-to-improve-incident-response; https://arxiv.org/html/2403.04123v1]

- **Triage into action categories (decide/inform/auto-resolve) is a well-established pattern across domains.** Medical triage, helpdesk ticket systems, and defect management all converge on a small number of action-oriented categories rather than open-ended priority lists. The three-way split in FN-03 (decision items, informational items, auto-resolve items) maps to this universal pattern. The key invariant: the classification must be based on the type of action required, not the severity of the issue. A critical issue may be auto-resolvable; a minor issue may require a strategic decision. — [First principles: action-type classification is orthogonal to severity classification. Conflating them (e.g., "high severity = needs discussion") creates false mappings. A gap in documentation is high-severity but auto-resolvable; a minor naming inconsistency between two capabilities may require a strategic naming decision.]

### Validated Assumptions

- **Single-pass synthesis is viable for the expected input size but carries known quality risks.** The feature spec mandates a single Claude invocation for the full report. Research shows that decomposed reasoning approaches significantly outperform single-pass for complex multi-document tasks. However, the coherence-report's input is already pre-structured (finding cards with typed categories, severity, and capability references), which reduces the reasoning burden compared to raw multi-document synthesis. For projects with fewer than ~20 findings, single-pass is likely adequate. For larger finding sets, quality degradation is expected, particularly in later sections. — [https://galileo.ai/blog/multi-context-processing-llms; https://arxiv.org/html/2410.13961v1]

- **LLMs will generate plausible-sounding synthesis even when source material contains no actual issues (zero-findings case).** GPT-3.5-Turbo generates summaries about non-existent subtopics 79.35% of the time; GPT-4o does so 44% of the time. The zero-findings clean-bill-of-health path must be enforced structurally (if finding_count == 0, skip synthesis), not left to the LLM's judgment. An LLM given project context and asked to synthesize recommendations will fabricate issues even when none exist in the finding cards. — [https://arxiv.org/html/2410.13961v1]

### Domain Risks

- **End-of-output quality degradation threatens the most critical sections.** The resolution sequence (section 5) and contradictions (section 6) are the sections most directly consumed by refinement-qa for agenda shaping. They are also the sections generated last in a single-pass approach, where hallucination rates are highest. This creates a structural risk: the most actionable output has the lowest reliability. Mitigation options include reordering sections (put contradictions and resolution sequence first) or having the orchestrator validate key sections post-generation. — [https://arxiv.org/html/2410.13961v1]

- **Conservative contradiction detection means the Q&A agenda will systematically underweight conflicts.** Since LLMs are high-precision/low-recall on contradictions, the Q&A agenda's "decision items" bucket (which should capture contradictions) will be smaller than reality warrants. Users may exit Q&A believing all contradictions have been addressed when undetected ones remain. The refinement-qa feature should be aware of this limitation and probe for additional contradictions beyond what RECOMMENDATIONS.md surfaces. — [https://arxiv.org/html/2504.00180v1]

- **Root cause grouping without explicit clustering instructions will produce symptom lists, not causal analysis.** The agent prompt for gsd-coherence-synthesizer.md must explicitly instruct the model to cluster findings by shared causal mechanisms, not merely group them by topic or affected capability. Without this instruction, the "Root Causes" section will likely be a re-organized list of findings rather than a genuine causal analysis. The distinction matters: a topic group ("these 3 findings affect the auth capability") is less actionable than a causal group ("these 3 findings all stem from no team having defined the auth contract"). — [First principles: LLMs default to surface-level organization (topic similarity) unless explicitly prompted for causal reasoning. Igor (CCS 2021) demonstrates the magnitude of this difference -- surface dedup produces 10x more "root causes" than causal clustering.]
